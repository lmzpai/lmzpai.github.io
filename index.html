Hi, I am Mengzhen Liu, a Ph.D. student at Peking University advised by Prof. Shanghang Zhang. My research centers on Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models and embodied agents, with a focus on building robotic systems that can perceive, reason, and act robustly in open-world environments.

My recent work forms a coherent VLA research pipeline spanning model architecture, data construction, perception enhancement, and efficient multimodal inference.

  • VLA Frameworks: RoboMamba and HybridVLA for end-to-end robotic reasoning and action generation.
  • VLA Data: RoboMIND and MotionTrans for multi-embodiment and human-to-robot manipulation learning.
  • Perception-Enhanced VLA: SaPaVe, RoboTracer, RoboBrain 2.5, and CordViP for active perception, spatial tracing, temporal grounding, and visuomotor perception.
  • Efficient VLM/VLA: Beyond Attention or Similarity (CDPruner) for training-free token pruning and deployable embodied intelligence.

I am always open to academic and industrial collaborations. Feel free to reach out if our interests align.
